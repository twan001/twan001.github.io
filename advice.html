    Coping With A New Environment

  • Fly or drive home if possible
    • Don't go home too often as you may fall behind in school
  • Call your friends and family
    • Facetime
    • Skype
    • Test
  • Go meet hallmates
    • Attend hall events and meetings
    • Knock on their doors and say hi
    • Do not be shy
  • Join Clubs
  • Rush a fraternity/sorority
  • Keep yourself occupied with anything
  • Be open minded
    • College is all about learning from mistakes
    • Give any opportunities a chance, but be smart
  • Do not live with regrets

    Dealing With Roommates

  • Try and live with someone you know
    • Easier to talk to and negotiate
    • Adds more comfortability
  • Choose roommates with similar living habits
    • Sleep schedule
    • Cleanliness
    • Light or heavy sleeper
    • etc.
  • Be CONSIDERATE!
  • Always talk out problems
  • If you are feeling edgy, feel free to blindy choose a roommate

    Classes

  • Buy books after the first day of class
    • Some classes might not use the book
    • Find free online PDFs
    • Ask friends for their books
    • Rent off of Amazon
  • Do not skip class
  • Attend Professors'/TAs' office hours
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help
  • Form study groups with friends

    How to Study For Finals

  • Start Early!!!!!
    • Quarter system goes by fast
    • After midterms, in a blink of an eye, it's finals week
    • Ideally start studying week 7 or 8
  • Go ask professor or TA's question
  • Study groups are super helpful
  • Do not procrastinate

    Transfer Guide

  • Go to every school you want to transfer to and find transfer requirements
  • Get a list of major classes and general education classes the school requires
  • Compare that list with your school's cirriculumn
  • Use assist.org
  • Do not fail classes
  • 3.5 or above GPA
  • Anything is possible. Good luck
